Wasif Ahmad Siddiqui

A Torch Bearer of Sir Syed Ahmed Khan's mission

Wasif Ahmad Siddiqui (1927 - 2020)

We witnessed the end of an era in 2020. Wasif Ahmad Siddiqui was the embodiment of a perfect balance in professional and family life. He was a loving husband, father, grandfather, great-grandfather, and a professional leader. He will be a role-model throughout our lives. May Allah bless him and grant him Jannah.

Education

  • 1930 : Recieved primary and secondary education in a village school established by his father.
  • 1952 : Bachelor's in Physics, Mathematics, and Geology from Aligarh Muslim University.
  • 1954 : Masters in Geophysics from Banaras Hindu University.
  • 1959 : Postgraduate degree in Petroleum Reservoir Engineering from Imperial College, London.
  • 1965 : Masters in Engineering Geology from the University of New Mexico, USA.
  • 1960 - 1970 : Member of Management committee of Tarbela High School.
  • 1980s : Member of the Management committee of Muscat Pakistani School in Oman.
  • 1990 - 2020 : Served as the Secratary Education Committee and as the Honorary Secretary of the Tehzibul Akhlaq Trust.

Professional

  • 1956 - 1975 : He contributed in Oil and Gas Exploration in Sindh and Makran regions of Pakistan. He was part of the Project Consultant team of Tarbela Dam Project. He provided special input to the Pakistan Army on Karakoram Highway project and Lowari Tunnel. He also played a major role in the feasibility study of Kalabagh Dam.
  • 1976 - 1980 : He executed various transportation projects in Oman.
  • 1981 - 1984 : He was part of Kalabagh Consultants on the detailed design of Kalabagh Dam.
    • 1984 - 1990 : He was engaged by the United Nations Development Fund (UNDP) and was appointed to the Ministry of Communications in Saudi Arabia. He made contributions to major projects in the transportation network around Holy Ka'aba in Mecca. He also had contributions to highways to Yemen passing through Bahah and Abha region.
  • 1990 - 2009 : He worked with Associated Consulting Engineers (ACE) as a Chief Geologist on various projects including Kurram Tangi Dam, Beris Dam Malaysia, and Lakhpass Tunnel Balochistan. Later on, he worked with BAK Consultants Engineers on Gandaf and Baja Tunnels and with Barqab on Laqi Tunnel, Chitral.
  • 2009 - 2020 : He worked with MM Pakistan (MMP) as Associate / Advisor Geology on various projects including Bunji Dam and Kurram Tangi Dam, and Sukhi Kinari Hydropower Project.
