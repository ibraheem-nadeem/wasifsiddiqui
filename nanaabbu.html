Wasif Ahmed Siddiqui

The man who created Pakistan's Geographical Engineering History

Wasif Ahmed Siddiqui (1927 - 2020)

Education

  • 1930 : Attended primary and secondary school in a village school.
  • 1952 : Bachelor's in Physics, Mathematics, and Geology from Aligarh Muslim University.
  • 1954 : Masters in Geophysics from Banaras Hindu University.
  • 1959 : Postgraduate degree in Petroleum Reservoir Engineering from Imperial College, London.
  • 1965 : Masters in Engineering Geology from the University of New Mexico, USA.
  • 1960 - 70s : Member of Management committee of Tarbela High School.
  • 1980s : Member of Management committee of Muskat Pakistani School in Oman.
  • 1990 - 2020 : Served as Secratary Education Committee and as the Honorary Secretary of the Tehzibul Akhlaq Trust founded by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan.

Professional

  • 1956 - 1975 : Oil and Gas Exploration in Sindh and Makran regions of Pakistan. Part of the Project Consultant team of Tarbela Dam Project. Provided special input to Pakistan Army on Karakoram Highway project and Lowari Tunnel. Played a major role in the feasibility study of Kalabagh Dam.
  • 1976 - 1980 : Various transportation projects in Oman.
  • 1981 - 1984 : Part of Kalabagh Consultants and worked in the detailed design of the Kalabagh Dam.
    • 1984 - 1990 : Engaged by the United Nations Development Fund (UNDP) and appointed to the Ministry of Communications in Saudi Arabia. Contributions to major developments in the transportation network around Holy Ka'aba in Mecca. Contributions to highways to Yemen passing through Bahah and Abha region.
  • 1990 - 2009 : Worked with Associated Consulting Engineers (ACE) on various projects including Kurram Tangi Dam, Beris Dam Malaysia, and Lakhpass Tunnel Balochistan. Worked with BAK Consultants Engineers on Gandaf and Baja Tunnels and with Barqab on Laqi Tunnel, Chitral.
  • 2009 - 2020 : Worked with MM Pakistan (MMP) on various projects including Bunji Dam and Kurram Tangi Dam, and Sukhi Kinari Hydropower Project.
